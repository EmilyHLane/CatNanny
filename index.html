Meet the Cat Nanny

There have been cats in my family for the majority of my life, always adopted from a shelter, and usually two at a time. A few years ago, I decided to simplify my life and started my snall usiness of being the cat nanny.

My pets right now are Ashe and Shadow, and when I go on vacation, I need to know that they are taken are of adn are comfortable with their sitter who gives them special attention. This is what I will provide for your pets. You will be able to go on your work travel or vacation knowing that I am there taking care of your pets, following your routine adn trying to make their lives as happy and comfortable as if you were there yourself.

Once I have taken care of someone's cats, they typically call on me several times a year, it seems to give them a secure feeling knowing that I'm there for them at their pets. I get great satisfaction from what I'm doing right now. Cats are such special animals with their own distinct personalities and truly fill my life with joy.

SPCA Volunteer Cat Socializer

I volunteered as a cat socializer in Shelter Medicine at the SF SPCA for 4 years. I have had trining in working with shy/undersocialized kittens and cats, affection eaters, and cats with aggressive behaviour or who get overstimulated easitly. I have a very soft voice and gentle approach and am usually very successful in coaxing out even the shyest cat.

Process

The first step I take as your cat nanny is a complimentary visit to introduce myself to your cat and learn more about what's needed to care for your cat. We'll review my cat care checklist that includes:

  • Your cat's personality traits
  • Dietary requirements and feeding schedule
  • Favorite toys and comfort items
  • Emergency and vet contact information
